The short strangle is one of the most powerful strategies in premium selling because it does not require you to pick a direction. You sell a put below the stock and a call above it, collect credit on both sides, and let time and volatility do the work. Jim Schultz builds one live on Starbucks from scratch, covering every number from strike selection to buying power to account size requirements.

The setup uses the 16 delta on both sides in the July cycle at 39 days. Starbucks at $95 makes it accessible for most account sizes. You need at least $20,000 to trade an undefined risk position like this responsibly. Managing winners comes at 50 percent of max profit. Managing losers on a short strangle is coming in the next episode.
