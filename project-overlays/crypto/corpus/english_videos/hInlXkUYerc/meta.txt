Delta is the most versatile Greek in options trading and also the most misunderstood. Finance classes teach it one way. Actual traders use it two other ways far more often. Jim Schultz breaks down all three in episode 8 of Where Do I Start.

At trade entry, delta is a probability estimate telling you the approximate chance that a strike expires in the money. Over the life of a position, delta is a share equivalent telling you how much directional exposure you are actually carrying. The textbook definition, how much the option price moves per dollar in the stock, is the one traders use least. All three are explained with live examples on Apple in the tastytrade platform, including how delta changes as time passes and as options move in and out of the money.
